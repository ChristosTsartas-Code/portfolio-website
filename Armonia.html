ARMONIA MENTAL HEALTH WEB APP

A responsive health and fitness application was developed by conducting a thorough analysis of the problem space and performing a competitive analysis. User research was conducted, and user personas, user flows, and user journey maps were created to ensure the application met user needs. Finally, a high-fidelity prototype was built and testing was conducted to ensure its effectiveness and usability.

Armonia Web App

Overview

Armonia is designed to provide users with the opportunity to manage their mental health and well-being. Whether you're struggling with stress, anxiety, depression, or simply looking to improve your overall mental health, Armonia can be a helpful companion on your journey. Features like guided meditations, mindfulness exercises, and sleep aids, can help you reduce stress, improve sleep quality, and build resilience over time. Whether you have just a few minutes to spare or want to create a daily self-care routine, Armonia can be a convenient and accessible way to prioritize your mental health and well-being.

Problem Contet
  • Our mental health application users need a way to address their mental health issues proactively and confidentially because they may not feel comfortable reaching out to a professional yet and want to explore self-help options first. We will know this to be true when we see an increase in the number of users engaging with the self-help features of the application and a decrease in the waiting time for appointments with professionals.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

In order to better understand the market landscape for mental health apps, I conducted a thorough competitive analysis by identifying and analyzing the strengths and weaknesses of two key competitors. Using a SWOT analysis, I gathered information on their products, pricing, marketing strategies, target audience, and customer feedback. By conducting this analysis, I gained valuable insights into how these competitors are positioning themselves in the market and identifying opportunities to differentiate my own mental health app. By understanding their strengths and weaknesses, I can develop strategies to capitalize on my own strengths and address any potential weaknesses.

Research findings

The two platforms, despite their strong market presence, have a few limitations: The talent pool on these platforms is limited, with a shortage of coaches, trainers, and other experts available. Expensive premium versions are available. The platforms have a strong market presence but have a few limitations. Users have trust issues with online platforms when it comes to dealing with mental health issues.

User Interviews

To gain insight into user behavior, I conducted interviews with primary users, asking open-ended questions and recording notes. Subsequently, I analyzed the gathered data and insights, organizing them through the use of affinity mapping.

User Outcome

A primary concern regarding expert recommendations is the preference for personal recommendations over online tools. Most users trust experts recommended by a trusted friend. Additionally, the price of such services should be reasonable, and the availability of free material should be sufficient for users to evaluate the app's value. Effective UX design should be simple and self-explanatory. Furthermore, such apps should offer course-like material to educate users. Users are willing to pay for services if they perceive a need and are comfortable with the app.
